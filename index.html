About

I am a PhD candidate in Sustainable Finance at Africa Business School, University Mohammed VI Polytechnic in Morocco. My dissertation, "Essays on Climate Resilience, Climate Finance Impacts, and Market Mispricing in the Global South," examines how climate shocks transmit through economic systems and affect sovereign risk in developing economies.

My research employs advanced econometric methods including wavelet analysis, structural vector autoregression (SVAR), and Monte Carlo simulation to understand climate-finance dynamics. I focus particularly on how climate-induced economic shocks create ripple effects through sovereign bond markets in Global South economies.

Before pursuing doctoral studies, I spent over three years at OCP Africa in Nigeria as a Regional Field Project Manager and Research Agronomist, where I led agricultural development initiatives, managed field trials, and collaborated with local financial institutions on project financing. I also founded AgriBox SARL, a hydroponic startup in Morocco.

My background bridges environmental science and finance, combining practical experience in climate-smart agriculture with rigorous training in econometrics and sustainable finance.

Research

Working Paper

Climate Shocks, Sectoral Transmission, and Monetary Policy: Evidence from Food Manufacturing in the United Kingdom

Uke, O. D., Ben Amar, A., Gorelkina, O.

Examining how climate shocks transmit through sectoral channels, particularly food manufacturing versus agriculture, to affect inflation dynamics and monetary policy effectiveness in the UK.

Working Paper

Climate Shockwaves: A Wavelet Analysis of Sovereign Risk Under a Changing Climate in Developed and Developing Economies

Uke, O. D., Ben Amar, A., Gorelkina, O.

Examining time-frequency dynamics of how climate-induced economic shocks transmit through sovereign bond markets, comparing risk spillover patterns between developed and developing economies.

Working Paper

Building Resilience, Reducing Risk: A Systematic Review of Climate Resilience, Climate Finance Impacts, and Market Mispricing in the Global South

Uke, O. D., Ben Amar, A., Gorelkina, O.

A comprehensive systematic review synthesizing the literature on climate resilience investments, climate finance effectiveness, and asset mispricing in emerging markets.
